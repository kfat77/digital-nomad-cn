2. Destinations Requiring Extra Caution

These countries are popular with nomads but women should be extra vigilant:

  • India: Avoid solo travel; choose established nomad areas (Goa, Bangalore)
  • Brazil: Big cities are risky; choose smaller safer cities (Florianópolis)
  • South Africa: High crime rates; avoid nighttime outings; choose secure neighborhoods
  • Mexico: Regional variation is huge; avoid high-crime states; choose Mexico City Roma/Condesa or San Miguel
  • Colombia: Medellín is generally safe but avoid certain areas; don't display wealth

3. Solo Safety Checklist

  1. Share itinerary: Tell family/friends when you move; use Google Maps location sharing
  2. Accommodation: Female-only dorms, highly-rated Airbnbs, or coliving spaces
  3. Night strategy: Avoid walking alone late at night; use Grab/Uber; avoid isolated areas
  4. Valuables: No expensive jewelry; phone lanyard; separate cash and cards
  5. Trust instincts: Leave immediately if something feels wrong; don't worry about politeness
  6. Self-defense basics: Learn basic techniques; carry legal self-defense tools (alarm)
  7. Join women communities: Nomad Girl, Girls LOVE Travel, Facebook women nomad groups

4. Health & Medical

  • Reproductive health: Research contraceptive availability at destination; bring long-term prescriptions
  • Women's health checkups: Choose countries with quality healthcare (Japan, Singapore, Thailand private hospitals)
  • Mental health: Loneliness is stronger in mobile life; establish counseling resources (BetterHelp)

6. Insurance Recommendations

Solo women should get comprehensive coverage:

  • SafetyWing: Basic global insurance, from $45/month
  • World Nomads: Covers adventure activities, highly rated by female travelers
  • Cigna Global: Premium comprehensive coverage including mental health
  • Must include: Emergency medical evacuation, mental health, post-assault medical support
