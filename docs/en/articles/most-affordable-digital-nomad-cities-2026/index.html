Top 15 ranking based on cost of living data from 122 cities, with budget-friendly recommendations for cost-conscious nomads.

Cost of Living Index Explained

The cost of living index uses New York City (100) as the baseline. An index of 50 means the city is roughly half as expensive as NYC. Data covers rent, food, transport, entertainment, and coworking space costs.

Best Value for Money

Southeast Asia Champions: Chiang Mai, Da Nang, Bali, and Ho Chi Minh City offer extremely low costs with mature nomad communities. Comfortable living on $800-1,200/month.

Europe on a Budget: Tbilisi, Bucharest, Kraków, Prague, and Budapest are Europe's most affordable nomad cities at $1,200-1,800/month.

Latin America Rising: Medellín, Mexico City, and Buenos Aires attract budget nomads with low rent and rich food cultures.

Budget Planning Tip: Combine the cost of living index with the rent index. Chiang Mai, Da Nang, and Tbilisi all have rent indices below 20, making them ideal for long-term stays.
