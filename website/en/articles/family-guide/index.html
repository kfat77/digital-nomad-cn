Digital Nomad Family Guide: Traveling with Kids

Digital Nomad Guide · July 9, 2026 · Based on 60-country data

Traveling the world with kids as a digital nomad? It sounds crazy, but more families are doing it every year. The key is choosing the right destination, solving education, healthcare, and safety. This guide gives families actionable plans based on our 60-country data platform.

2. Education Solutions

International Schools

Most families choose international schools. Annual fees:

  • Southeast Asia (Thailand, Malaysia): $8,000-$20,000/year
  • Europe (Portugal, Spain): $6,000-$15,000/year
  • Japan/Singapore: $15,000-$35,000/year

Recommended platforms: International Schools Database, Good Schools Guide.

Homeschooling / Worldschooling

Some families choose homeschooling or "worldschooling": Worldschooling Hub, Edumenta offer curricula for traveling families. Best for elementary ages, requires one parent to invest significant time.

Local Public Schools

In Portugal, Spain, Japan, children with long-term visas can attend public schools for free. Lowest cost option, but requires local language proficiency.

3. Healthcare & Insurance

Family healthcare is one of the biggest expenses:

  • Global family health insurance: Cigna Global, Allianz Care, AXA — $3,000-$8,000/year
  • Local insurance: In Thailand, Malaysia — $1,000-$3,000/year
  • National healthcare: EU countries (Portugal, Spain) offer near-free public healthcare for residents

💡 Tip: At minimum, get insurance covering emergency medical evacuation. It can save your life.

5. Visa Strategy

Family visas are more complex but have advantages:

  • Portugal D7: Family application together, children attend public school
  • Spain Digital Nomad Visa: Allows spouse and children
  • Thailand Elite Visa: 5-20 years, ideal for long-term family planning
  • Malaysia MM2H: 10-year visa, family-friendly
  • Japan Business Manager Visa: Open a company, bring whole family, children attend public school

6. Practical Tips

  1. Test first: Travel with kids for 1-2 months before committing to long-term stays
  2. Stability matters: Stay at least 6 months per location to build social circles for children
  3. Co-parenting: One parent focuses on work, the other on education and daily life
  4. Keep Chinese language: Maintain Chinese reading and writing with online resources
  5. Family rituals: Fixed dinner times, weekend activities create stability amid mobility
