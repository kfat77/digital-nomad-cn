Digital Nomad Insurance Guide: Health, Equipment, Travel

Digital Nomad Guide · July 8, 2026 · Based on 60-country data

When you're moving between countries, insurance is no longer optional—it's essential. One unexpected medical bill can wipe out six months of savings, and a stolen laptop can bring your work to a halt. This guide helps you systematically plan your insurance coverage.

1. Insurance Types Every Digital Nomad Needs

  • 🩺 Health Insurance: Covers medical expenses in your primary destinations
  • 💻 Equipment Insurance: Covers laptop, phone, camera, and other work equipment
  • ✈️ Travel Insurance: Flight cancellation, baggage delay, emergency evacuation, personal liability

3. Healthcare Quality vs Insurance Needs

Based on our 60-country data, healthcare quality and insurance needs vary significantly:

  • High quality + insurance required (USA, Switzerland): Extremely expensive, insurance is mandatory, premiums $200+
  • High quality + insurance optional (Thailand, Malaysia): Private hospitals affordable, basic insurance sufficient
  • Medium quality + insurance required (Georgia, Colombia): Limited public healthcare, private or evacuation needed
  • Low quality + insurance required (some African countries): Any serious condition requires evacuation

4. Equipment Insurance

For digital nomads, a laptop is a lifeline. Equipment insurance typically covers:

  • Accidental damage (drops, liquid spills)
  • Theft (requires police report)
  • Loss (some policies cover)
  • Global warranty extension

💡 Tip: AppleCare+ (Mac/iPhone), Dell ProSupport, or third-party providers like Cover Genius offer global coverage. Some travel insurance (e.g., World Nomads) includes equipment protection.

5. Digital Nomad Visa Insurance Requirements

Most digital nomad visas require proof of valid health insurance. Common requirements:

  • Minimum coverage: €30,000 - €50,000 (Schengen typically requires €30,000)
  • Coverage area: Must include the visa country
  • Duration: Must cover entire visa period

6. Insurance Recommendations by Budget

Budget: SafetyWing (best value for money)

Comprehensive: Cigna Global (best medical network and claims experience)

Short-term: World Nomads (good for initial exploration phase)

Long-term base: Local private insurance (usually 50% cheaper than international)
