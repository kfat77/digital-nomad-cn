Digital Nomad Communities: Meetups, Coworking & Online Groups

Digital Nomad Guide · July 9, 2026 · Based on 60-country data

The biggest challenge for digital nomads isn't work—it's loneliness. No colleagues, no fixed social circle, no sense of belonging. Community is the antidote. This guide helps you find global digital nomad communities, from online to offline, from free to paid.

2. Recommended Coworking by City

Asia

  • Canggu, Bali: Dojo Bali, Tropical Nomad — highest nomad density globally
  • Chiang Mai: Punspace, Yellow — low cost, high quality community
  • Bangkok: The Hive, WeWork — business atmosphere
  • Tokyo: WeWork, CoEdo — design-focused
  • Singapore: The Working Capitol, JustCo — premium business

Europe

  • Lisbon: Second Home, LACS — creative industry hub
  • Barcelona: MOB, WeWork — international vibe
  • Berlin: Betahaus, Factory Berlin — tech startup scene
  • Budapest: Kaptár, Loffice — great value
  • Tallinn: Lift99, Spring Hub — startup ecosystem

Americas

  • Mexico City: WeWork, Homework — largest Latin American nomad community
  • Medellín: AtomHouse, Selina — rapid growth
  • Buenos Aires: La Maquinita, AreaTres — European feel

4. Community Engagement Strategy

  1. Find community first when arriving in a new city. Search Nomad List or Facebook for local groups.
  2. Online to offline: Connect on Telegram/Slack first, then meet for coffee.
  3. Contribute value: Share your expertise, organize small events, become a community core.
  4. Don't only hang with nomads: Integrate with locals, learn the language, experience real culture.
  5. Maintain long-term relationships: High mobility means you need to actively keep in touch.
